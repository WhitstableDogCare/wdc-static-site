Staff Illness and Injury Policy

As a husband-and-wife business, the health and wellbeing of Jack and Kim Rojas Powell is central to the safe operation of Whitstable Dog Care.

Minor Illness (One Person Unwell)

  • The unwell person will rest, remain away from the dog areas, and avoid direct contact with dogs and clients.
  • The well person will assume full responsibility for all dog care duties.
  • If the well person cannot safely manage the current number of dogs alone, affected clients will be contacted as early as possible to arrange early collection or defer the booking.
  • No new bookings will be accepted while operating single-handedly if doing so would exceed what one person can safely manage.
  • If the unwell person has a contagious illness (e.g. vomiting, diarrhoea), they will not handle food, water bowls, or bedding until symptom-free for at least 48 hours.

Serious Illness or Injury (One Person Fully Incapacitated)

  1. Client notification: All clients with dogs currently in our care, or booked imminently, will be contacted as soon as practicable.
  2. Capacity reduction: No new bookings accepted during this period unless safe, high-quality care can be maintained by one person alone.
  3. Existing guests: If the remaining person cannot safely manage alone, clients will be asked to collect their dogs as a priority. If a client cannot collect, we will arrange care at a local licensed boarding establishment — our preferred contact is Milo & Co (informal arrangement).
  4. Emergency cover: If dogs cannot be collected and no licensed alternative is available at short notice, Amie Powell (07966 045 773) may be contacted as a last resort.
  5. Canterbury City Council: We will notify Canterbury City Council if the situation affects our ability to operate the licence for more than a short period.
  6. Return to full operation: Only once the incapacitated person has recovered sufficiently to resume duties safely.

Both of Us Unwell or Incapacitated

  1. All owners or their named buddies will be contacted immediately and asked to collect their dogs as a matter of urgency.
  2. If an owner or buddy cannot collect, we will arrange for the dog to be transferred to a local licensed boarding establishment — our preferred contact is Milo & Co (informal arrangement).
  3. If dogs cannot be placed and immediate on-site cover is needed, Amie Powell (07966 045 773) will be contacted to provide emergency assistance.
  4. Canterbury City Council will be informed and kept updated.
  5. All future bookings will be paused until normal operation can safely resume.

Injury During Dog Care

  1. Immediate first aid: First aid supplies are kept in the utility cupboard in the dog area. Seek medical attention if required — do not delay for dog care.
  2. Secure the dogs: Before leaving to seek medical attention, ensure all dogs are safely secured.
  3. Contact the other person: The injured person should contact their partner immediately so cover can be arranged.
  4. Dog bites: Any dog bite that breaks the skin must be washed thoroughly with soap and water for at least five minutes and assessed by a medical professional. The incident must be recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app.
  5. Incident recording: All injuries must be recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app Incident Log, including date, time, nature of injury, and action taken.
  6. Review: Following any injury, review the circumstances and update procedures or risk assessment as appropriate.

Fire Safety

  • A fire extinguisher and fire blanket are kept in the kitchen.
  • Smoke detectors are located in the kitchen, living room, hall, and upstairs landing.
  • Entrances and fire exits are kept clear of obstructions.
  • On discovering a fire, immediately sound the alarm.
  • All dogs will be put on a lead — leads are kept in the Woof Shed and in the kitchen by the back door. Wait with all dogs outside 13 Walmer Road.
  • Owners will be notified to collect their dogs.
  • Dogs will only be returned to the house when it is safe to do so.
  • Dogs that can no longer be accommodated due to damage will be transferred to another licensed dog boarding establishment.
  • Fire drills are undertaken twice per year; one must involve the removal of all dogs from the house.

Carbon Monoxide

  • Gas boiler is located in the spare bedroom. A carbon monoxide detector is in the spare room and tested monthly. The boiler is serviced annually by a Gas Safe engineer.
  • If the carbon monoxide detector sounds: quickly make your way out of the building opening windows and doors as you go. Call 999. Do not re-enter the building until told it is safe to do so.

Loss of Power

  • Emergency torches are located in the kitchen near the back door, tested monthly.
  • Contact the power company to ascertain when power is likely to be restored.
  • If off for more than 24 hours, consider hiring a generator.
  • Loss of power in winter may lead to loss of heating — follow the Loss of Heating plan.
  • If power cannot be restored, arrange to board dogs in nearby licensed establishments.

Loss of Heating

  • Ensure all doors and windows are closed to prevent heat loss.
  • Consider alternative heating (gas heaters approved for indoor use only) — perform a risk assessment before use.
  • If temperature cannot be safely maintained above 10°C, arrange to board dogs in nearby licensed establishments.

Loss of Water

  • Contact the utility company to determine duration.
  • If less than 24 hours: purchase sufficient water bottles for drinking, handwashing, and spot cleaning.
  • If more than 24 hours: place dogs with other licensed boarding premises until water is re-established. Deep clean the premises when water is restored.

Premises Become Uninhabitable

  • Contact owner or named buddy first to care for their animal.
  • If unable to accommodate, place the dog in local licensed boarding establishments within 24 hours.
  • Inform Canterbury City Council when all dogs have been placed.
  • All owners will be informed of the name, address, and contact details of the establishment caring for their dog.

Extreme Weather — Hot Weather Policy

  • Ensure dogs always have access to warm, dry indoor environment and warm bedding.

Feeding Regimes

Water

  • Fresh water provided for each dog daily in separate water bowls.
  • Water changed once daily or sooner if visibly soiled or contaminated.
  • Water bowls kept separate from food bowls and positioned in several areas around the dog areas.
  • A water bowl is provided in any room a dog is confined to.

Food

  • All dietary requirements and allergy details are pre-requested on booking.
  • All dogs are fed separately. Baby gates are used to divide dogs at mealtimes to prevent food stealing and reduce stress.
  • Dogs showing signs of aggression when fed will always be fed separately.
  • All dogs fed at least one meal per day unless advised otherwise by owner or vet.
  • Feed times: 8am and 4pm.
  • Dry food is stored in the utility cupboard in the owner's own containers. Wet food is stored in the fridge.
  • Food intake monitored daily. If a dog has not eaten for a significant period, the owner will be contacted. Veterinary advice will be sought if the owner advises it or if other signs of illness are present.
  • Raw food is not accepted as standard due to the risk of cross-contamination. Exceptions may be considered on a case-by-case basis following discussion with the owner.

Cleaning Regimes

Cleaning Products

  • Safe4 Professional Disinfectant — veterinary-grade. Used as ready-to-use surface spray or concentrate: 1:100 for regular cleaning, 1:50 for hard surface disinfection.
  • Safe4 Hypoallergenic Laundry Detergent — used for all laundry (animal and human). 5ml–20ml per kilo of dry weight.
  • K9 Turf Bio Enzyme Cleaner — for artificial grass. Dilution 1:10 to 1:5. Used weekly or twice weekly depending on usage and weather.

Safety Data Sheets are held alongside products. All products stored in their original containers in a cool, dry, secure location inaccessible to children and animals. Storage temperature: 0–35°C.

Cleaning Procedure

  1. Remove dogs from the area before cleaning.
  2. Physically remove debris (blue roll, dog waste bags). Sweep or hoover before applying solutions.
  3. Clean top to bottom (walls, ceilings, floors).
  4. Allow disinfectants the recommended contact time.
  5. Follow correct dilutions and rinse thoroughly where advised.
  6. Ensure adequate ventilation when using cleaning products.
  7. Ensure all surfaces are completely dry before allowing dogs back in.

Transportation

  • Whitstable Dog Care owns one vehicle: a Skoda Enyaq (family car, also used for non-business use and our own dogs).
  • Dogs may be transported to/from the vet and to/from an owner's home if needed.
  • Dogs are never left alone in the vehicle.
  • Any journey over 20 minutes is broken up with water and toilet breaks.
  • All pickups and collections include dogs on leads between homes and vehicle.
  • The boot has a Skoda boot divider. Two dogs can be transported at one time.
  • Vehicle is cleaned and disinfected as required using a vacuum cleaner and Safe4 disinfectant.

Vehicle Breakdown with Dogs in Transit

Whitstable Dog Care maintains comprehensive breakdown cover with RAC.

  1. Pull over to the safest possible location away from traffic. Turn on hazard lights.
  2. Call the breakdown service immediately and state that dogs are in the vehicle.
  3. Ensure adequate ventilation, offer water if safe, and stay with dogs at all times.
  4. Monitor dog temperatures closely in extreme heat or cold.
  5. Notify owners immediately.
  6. Follow breakdown service advice, prioritising dog safety and wellbeing.

Prevention and Control of Disease

Ill Health

  • No animal suffering from, or suspected to be suffering from, any infectious or contagious disease can be accepted.
  • Daily observations are made to check for signs of illness.
  • Should an animal in our care show signs of infectious or contagious disease they will be immediately transferred to isolation facilities, the owner's designated vet, or Whitstable Bay Veterinary Centre.
  • The dog will be treated according to the agreements in the consent form.
  • The home and vehicles are cleaned according to the cleaning regime.

Vaccinations

All dogs must be vaccinated against:

  • Canine parvovirus
  • Canine distemper
  • Infectious canine hepatitis (adenovirus)
  • Leptospirosis

We also ask that dogs are vaccinated against:

  • Bordetella bronchiseptica
  • Canine parainfluenza virus

Owners are asked to provide an up-to-date vaccination record prior to their dog's stay. For established clients with a known history, we may accept a booking on trust at our discretion.

Fleas and Worming

All clients are asked to worm and flea treat their dogs within 1 week prior to boarding. If there is evidence of fleas, lice, ticks, or worms during a stay, we will contact the client's vet to arrange treatment. The cost of any treatment will be invoiced to the client.

Handwashing

  1. All staff wash their hands when they start work, before interacting with any dogs.
  2. All staff wash their hands after interacting with dogs.
  3. All staff wash their hands after undertaking any cleaning jobs.
  4. Hand wipes and sanitiser are provided when walking dogs away from the home.
  5. A clean hand drying towel is provided daily or more frequently if soiled.

Infection Control for Dogs in Isolation

  • If a dog shows signs of an infectious disease, Whitstable Dog Care will immediately contact the owner and ask for the dog to be collected.
  • The upstairs spare bedroom is prepared as the isolation room: rug lifted, laminate floor cleaned, penned area with puppy pads and fresh water, small window opened for ventilation.
  • A lead is kept in the isolation room to take the dog down the stairs and out of the front door to toilet on a puppy pad — preventing cross contamination with the dog area at the back of the house.
  • PPE (disposable gloves, disposable apron, impermeable shoe covers) is stored in both the upstairs and downstairs utility cupboards. PPE must be worn by anyone entering the isolation room, removed before leaving, and discarded after a single use.
  • Items in the isolation room are treated as infectious and must be bagged before removal, then cleaned and disinfected or disposed of in external bins.
  • Hands must be washed immediately after leaving isolation. If clothing becomes soiled it must be changed and washed before the staff member interacts with other dogs.
  • Once the dog has been collected, the isolation room is cleaned thoroughly using PPE and Safe4 disinfectant.

Monitoring the Health and Welfare of All Dogs

Dogs are monitored continuously throughout the day. Observations are recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app. Routine observations are recorded for all dogs on trial and whenever something appears out of the ordinary, including changes in appetite, behaviour, toileting, or temperament.

If there is concern for the health or safety of a dog, more frequent observations will be recorded. Each entry should include date, time, observations, decisions made, and the name of the staff member who made them.

Enrichment

Daily enrichment tailored to each individual dog's needs:

  • Human interactions such as play time or grooming
  • Interactions with other dogs
  • Two 30-minute walks per day
  • Time in the outside dog area
  • Puzzle feeders
  • Access to a variety of dog toys

Veterinary Interventions

  • If we believe a dog requires vet treatment, we will first try to contact the owner or named buddy (except in emergencies).
  • We will always endeavour to take the dog to its usual vet; if not possible, to Whitstable Bay Veterinary Centre.
  • If expensive treatments are required, we will again try to contact the owner or named buddy.
  • If they cannot be contacted, we will take advice from the vet and make decisions based on the health and welfare of the dog.
  • If euthanasia is recommended on humane grounds by the vet, we will contact the owner or named buddy for consent. No euthanasia will take place without owner consent. Records are kept of any euthanasia, including the vet who carried it out.

Medicines

  • All medicines administered according to the instructions of the owner or vet.
  • All medication stored safely in the medicine container in the utility cupboard.
  • Every administration must be recorded in the Medication Log — a dedicated paper notebook kept alongside the medications in the utility cupboard. Each entry must include: dog's name, name of medication, dose given, date and time, and initials of the person who administered it. The log must be completed immediately after each administration.

The Death or Escape of a Dog

Escape of a Dog

Every effort is made to ensure that the home is secure and dogs cannot escape. All boarding dogs must wear a collar at all times. Whitstable Dog Care attaches a tag to the collar with the premises name and phone number. Windows that open to non-gated areas are kept closed or secured with shutters at all times.

In the unlikely event that a dog does escape:

  1. All available staff make an immediate search of the Whitstable Dog Care premises and grounds.
  2. The Dog Warden Service is contacted and details of the dog left with them.
  3. Local vets are contacted to see if the dog has come to their attention.
  4. The owner and named buddy are contacted and kept informed. If possible, the home address is checked in case the dog has returned home.
  5. Posters are placed and local streets searched.
  6. Social media posts and groups are used asking local residents to share a photograph and report sightings.
  7. A thorough investigation is undertaken and procedures updated as needed. The incident is recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app.

Death of a Dog

In the unlikely event of the death of a dog:

  • The dog will be transported to Whitstable Bay Vets or the owner's veterinary practice.
  • If not immediately possible, the dog will be transferred to the Woof Shed (which is locked) until the owner/buddy or the registered/local veterinary practice can collect.
  • The vet will be asked to determine the cause of death.
  • The owner and/or named buddy will be contacted at the earliest opportunity.
  • The dog's body will be stored at the vet until the owner is able to collect, or gives instructions for disposal.
  • The incident is recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app.
  • No one other than a vet may euthanise. No euthanasia will take place without owner consent.
  • Records are kept of any euthanasia along with the name of the vet who carried it out.

New Dogs

Before agreeing a paid trial, owners and their dogs come to Whitstable Dog Care for a free Meet & Greet in the indoor/outdoor dog area. Using gates and leads to give the dog plenty of space to meet existing dogs, owners and staff monitor behaviour and discuss requirements. At the end of the Meet & Greet, a verbal agreement is made about whether a trial will go ahead.

Whitstable Dog Care will then agree to a paid day or boarding trial without the owner present. If no issues arise the dog is able to stay and/or board with us. All new dogs are closely monitored during their trial, with observations and any incidents recorded in the Whitstable Dog Care app.

Neutering and Intact Dogs

Whitstable Dog Care accepts both neutered and intact (entire) dogs, subject to the conditions below. Neutering status is recorded on the Booking & Consent Form and reconfirmed at each booking.

Owner Responsibilities

  • Owners must declare neutering status on the Booking & Consent Form. Any change must be communicated before arrival.
  • For intact females, owners must inform us of the date of the last season and the expected date of the next season.
  • Recently neutered dogs must not be brought to board or daycare until a vet has confirmed full recovery, sutures removed or dissolved, and cleared for normal activity. Minimum 14 days post-procedure recommended.

Intact Females

  • Not accepted while in season, nor in the 7 days before or after a known or expected season.
  • If an intact female unexpectedly comes into season during a stay, the owner or named buddy will be contacted immediately. The dog will be separated, kept in a designated room, and walked separately on lead at quiet times.
  • Cancellation due to a female coming into season before a confirmed booking is treated under the standard Cancellation Policy.

Intact Males

  • Intact males up to and including 12 months of age are accepted subject to a successful Meet & Greet and trial.
  • Intact males over 12 months of age are not normally accepted. Exceptions may be made at the discretion of Whitstable Dog Care for established clients with a known behavioural history, or where neutering has been delayed on veterinary advice. Written confirmation from the dog's vet may be requested.
  • During a stay, intact males may be fed, walked, and rested separately from other dogs at our discretion.

Puppies

  • We do not require dogs to be neutered. Owners who choose not to neuter are asked to discuss management expectations at the Meet & Greet.
  • Puppies must be fully vaccinated before any stay, regardless of neutering status.

Records

  • Neutering status, date of last/expected season for intact females, and any related notes are recorded via the Booking & Consent Form, which feeds into the Whitstable Dog Care app and is reviewed at every booking.
  • Any incident relating to an in-season female or behaviour by an intact male is recorded in the app and, where appropriate, as a formal incident.

Drop-Off & Collection Procedure

Ringing the doorbell unsettles the dogs, so we ask all clients to follow this procedure:

  1. Please use the side gate and do not ring the front doorbell.
  2. Send us a WhatsApp message to let us know you have arrived.
  3. Keep your dog on the lead and wait outside the gate — we will come out to meet you.
  4. If you have not heard from us within a few minutes, please call us.

Licence Revocation

If the licence is suspended or revoked by Canterbury City Council, Whitstable Dog Care is no longer allowed to board dogs.

  • The owner or their named buddy will be contacted first to care for their animal.
  • If unable to accommodate, arrangements will be made to place the dog in local licensed boarding establishments — our preferred contact is Milo & Co (informal arrangement) — within 24 hours.
  • Canterbury City Council will be informed when all dogs have been placed.
  • All dog owners or their named buddies will be informed of the name, address, and contact details of the establishment caring for their dog.
  • All future bookings will be cancelled and owners informed.
